FAQ
Plants produce oxygen through the process of photosynthesis, where they use sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to create glucose and release oxygen as a byproduct.
The main parts of a plant include roots, which anchor and absorb water and nutrients; stems, which support and transport materials; leaves, where photosynthesis occurs; and flowers, which are involved in reproduction.
Plants adapt through various mechanisms, such as developing thick, waxy skins to retain water in deserts, air-filled tissues to float in aquatic environments, and large leaves to capture more sunlight in dense forests.
